### Website Requirements Brief

## Call Brad

Purpose: give the developer a clear brief for rebuilding callbrad.eu as a modern landing page that keeps the brand feel, shifts the commercial focus, and is easy to update going forward.

1. Project summary
The current website is visually clean and already uses a simple landing-page structure with sections such as expertise, about, pricing, and contact. The new website should keep that clarity, but with a much stronger commercial focus on products rather than general consultancy.
The website should move away from Wix and be rebuilt on a platform that is cheaper, easier to maintain, and faster to update.
The primary commercial focus should be on: (1) RoPA Collaborative System and (2) Employees GDPR Training. DPO services should remain visible and credible, but secondary.

The website should feel premium, trustworthy, modern, and minimal. It does not need complex functionality.

2. Main goals
• Present the business as a product-led privacy/compliance offering, not only a consultancy.
• Create a strong landing page that clearly explains the three offers: DPO services, RoPA Collaborative System, and Employees GDPR Training.
• Make RoPA and Training the hero products while still preserving DPO credibility.
• Allow easy future edits to product copy, package details, testimonials, and pricing.
• Improve maintainability, performance, and cost compared with Wix.

3. Target audience
Primary audience: startups and small-to-mid-sized businesses that need practical GDPR support, a collaborative RoPA/compliance system, or employee GDPR training without enterprise-software overhead.

4. Scope of work
• Design and build a single landing page website (plus required legal pages and contact pathways).
• Migrate away from Wix.
• Preserve brand continuity where useful, but substantially update services, structure, and pricing.
• Set up a content structure that lets the owner edit text and pricing later without rebuilding the site.
• Optimise for desktop and mobile.

5. Brand, visual direction, and UI rules
- Element	Requirement
- Overall feel	Beautiful, premium, modern, minimal, trustworthy, product-led.
- Background	White as the dominant background.
- Accent colours	#2b2e83 and #ed60ed.
- Title font	Montserrat.
- Body font	Roboto.
- Text colour on white	#222222.
- Text colour on coloured backgrounds	White.
- Design direction	Clean layouts, generous spacing, clear hierarchy, restrained use of colour, polished CTA blocks.

6. Information architecture and recommended page flow
- Hero section: Strong headline positioning the business around practical GDPR products and services. Primary CTA should point to booking/contact. Secondary CTA can point to learning about the products.
- Products overview: Three-card or three-block section for: RoPA Collaborative System, Employees GDPR Training, and DPO Services. RoPA and Training should be visually more prominent than DPO.
- RoPA Collaborative System: Dedicated section explaining the collaborative/privacy-workspace angle. Leave room for the owner to refine copy, features, pricing, and screenshots later.
- Employees GDPR Training: Dedicated section explaining the employee-training offer. Leave room for the owner to refine modules, delivery format, and pricing later.
- DPO Services: Dedicated section for DPO offer, including package pricing table and optional add-ons.
- Why this approach: Short section explaining why this is more practical than bloated tools or expensive consultancy-heavy setups.
- About / credibility: Short founder credibility section with photo, certifications, and concise trust markers.
- FAQ: Optional, but recommended for objections about pricing, scope, implementation, and who the products are for.
- Contact / CTA: Simple final CTA block with email, consultation booking, and/or enquiry form.
- Legal footer: Privacy Notice, company details, copyright.

7. Product priority rules
• RoPA Collaborative System and Employees GDPR Training must be the main commercial focus of the page.
• These two offers should receive the strongest visual weight after the hero section.
• DPO Services should still appear as an important, serious offer, but not dominate the page.
• The developer should structure the CMS/content blocks so the owner can later update the RoPA and Training descriptions, features, screenshots, testimonials, and pricing without major redesign.

8. DPO Services content and pricing
- The DPO section should include the following packages and add-ons. Pricing should be clearly visible and presented in a clean comparison layout.
- Package / add-on	Includes	Price
- Essential DPO	1 x 30 min monthly sync call; yearly high-level GDPR gap analysis; basic asynchronous support via email; appointed DPO for 1 entity in 1 jurisdiction	199 EUR/mo.

- Growth DPO	8 hours work/mo.; 2 x 30 min monthly sync calls; yearly in-depth GDPR gap analysis + prioritised action plan; priority asynchronous support via email and agreed business messaging channels; priority phone support for urgent privacy matters during business hours; appointed DPO for 1 entity in 1 jurisdiction	999 EUR/mo.
- Extra legal entity	Additional legal entity for DPO work	99+ EUR/mo.
- Extra jurisdiction registry	Additional jurisdiction for DPO work	49+ EUR/mo.
- Extra hourly rate	Additional hourly rate work (any work outside business hours)	150+ EUR/mo.

9. Placeholder requirements for RoPA and Training
• Both product sections must be designed in a way that already looks complete and premium, even if final pricing and some copy are still pending.
• Each section should support: headline, short explainer, benefits, feature list, example workflow/use case, optional screenshots or mockups, testimonial slot, pricing slot, CTA.
• The design should not break if pricing is temporarily shown as “Coming soon”, “From …”, or as a custom enquiry.
• The owner should be able to replace placeholder text later without redesigning the whole page.

10. Functional requirements
• Responsive design for desktop, tablet, and mobile.
• Fast-loading landing page.
• Easy editing for core copy and prices.
• Simple CTA pathways: consultation booking, email contact, and optional contact form.
• SEO basics: editable page title, meta description, clean headings, image alt text, and sensible page structure.
• Analytics-ready setup (for example, easy insertion of analytics and tracking tools).
• Legal pages/links preserved: Privacy Notice and company footer details.

11. Content migration notes from the current website
- The current site has a simple one-page structure with sections for expertise, about, pricing, and contact. This clarity is worth keeping.
- The current site is heavily framed around general privacy consultancy. The new site should instead be reframed around the three defined offers.
- The current site already contains credibility content (founder introduction, certifications, privacy expertise). This can be retained in a shorter and more polished format.
- Existing broad consultancy copy should be pruned and rewritten to match the new positioning.

12. Suggested deliverables from the developer
• One approved landing-page design.
• One fully built and responsive website.
• Migration off Wix.
• Editable content areas for product descriptions and pricing.
• Handover instructions for future edits.

13. Open items still to be finalised by owner
• Final copy and pricing for RoPA Collaborative System.
• Final copy and pricing for Employees GDPR Training.
• Final CTA wording.
• Decision on final platform/stack, subject to developer recommendation.

Priority reminder: the new website is not meant to be a generic consultancy brochure. It should act as a strong product-led landing page in which the RoPA Collaborative System and Employees GDPR Training are the main offers, while DPO Services remain an important secondary offer.
